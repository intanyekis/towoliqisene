Solasta Crown of the Magister wiki

Solasta: Crown of the Magister is a turn-based tactical role-playing game developed by Tactical Adventures and released in 2021. The game takes place in a high-fantasy world where magic and mythical creatures are commonplace. Players take on the role of a party of four adventurers who must complete quests, battle monsters, and unravel the mysteries of the world.

The gameplay of Solasta: Crown of the Magister is reminiscent of classic tactical RPGs like Baldur’s Gate and Neverwinter Nights. Players control their party of adventurers from a top-down perspective, moving them around the game world and engaging in turn-based combat with enemies. The combat system is deeply strategic, allowing players to use a variety of abilities and spells to gain the upper hand in battles.

One of the most unique features of Solasta: Crown of the Magister is its focus on verticality. The game world is full of high cliffs, deep pits, and other obstacles that players must navigate in order to succeed. This adds an extra layer of complexity to the gameplay, as players must carefully consider the positioning of their characters in order to gain a tactical advantage.

In addition to the challenging combat and exploration, Solasta: Crown of the Magister features a deep and engaging storyline. The game world is filled with interesting characters and factions, each with their own motivations and agendas. Players must navigate the complex political landscape of the world in order to complete their quests and achieve their goals.

Overall, Solasta: Crown of the Magister is a fantastic game for fans of tactical RPGs. Its deep combat system, focus on verticality, and engaging storyline make it a must-play for anyone who enjoys challenging and immersive games.

Solasta Crown of the Magister Walkthrough

Here’s a walkthrough for Solasta: Crown of the Magister:

Character Creation

Start by creating your four-person party. You can choose from four different races (Human, Elf, Dwarf, or Halfling) and four different classes (Fighter, Rogue, Cleric, or Wizard). Customize your party members with unique appearances, abilities, and backgrounds.

Tutorial

After creating your party, you’ll start the tutorial mission. This will introduce you to the basic mechanics of the game, including exploration, combat, and using skills. Pay close attention to the tutorial, as it will prepare you for the rest of the game.

Exploration

Solasta is a game that heavily emphasizes exploration. As you travel through the various environments, keep an eye out for hidden objects and treasure. You’ll also encounter various NPCs that will offer quests and provide valuable information.

Combat

Combat is turn-based in Solasta, and you’ll need to use tactics and strategy to defeat your enemies. Position your characters carefully, use cover to your advantage, and make use of your skills and spells to overcome your opponents. Be aware that enemies can also use cover and other tactics against you.

Magic

Magic plays a large role in Solasta. As you level up, your characters will gain access to more spells, which can be used both in and out of combat. Be sure to choose your spells carefully and consider how they can be used in conjunction with your other party members.

Quests

Solasta is full of side quests, which can be found by talking to NPCs or discovering clues in the environment. Completing these quests will not only earn you valuable rewards, but also help you gain experience and level up your characters.

Gear

As you progress through the game, you’ll find a variety of weapons, armor, and other gear. Be sure to equip your characters with the best gear available to increase their combat effectiveness.

Character Development

 As you gain experience and level up your characters, you’ll have the opportunity to choose new abilities, feats, and spells. Consider carefully which abilities will best suit your playstyle and party composition.

Party Composition

Finally, it’s important to consider the composition of your party. Each class has its own strengths and weaknesses, and you’ll need to choose party members that complement each other’s abilities. For example, a party with a Fighter, Rogue, Cleric, and Wizard can cover a wide range of combat and support abilities.

Overall, Solasta: Crown of the Magister is a deep and challenging RPG that rewards careful planning and strategy. By mastering the game’s mechanics and developing a strong party, you’ll be able to overcome even the toughest challenges and emerge victorious.

Solasta Crown of the Magister Guide

Here is a guide for Solasta Crown of the Magister that may help you get started on your journey:

Create your party

Before starting your adventure, you will need to create your party of four adventurers. Each character has different attributes, skills, and abilities, so make sure to create a balanced party that can handle various situations. A common recommendation is to have at least one character who is proficient in melee combat, one who is proficient in ranged combat, one who can heal, and one who can use magic.

Explore the world

Solasta: Crown of the Magister has an expansive world filled with secrets, treasures, and quests. Take your time to explore every nook and cranny, as you may find hidden items, secret paths, or new quests. Be mindful of the verticality of the game, as some areas may require you to climb or jump to reach.

Engage in turn-based combat

Combat in Solasta: Crown of the Magister is turn-based and strategic. Before engaging in a battle, assess your surroundings and plan your strategy accordingly. Use your party members’ abilities and spells to gain the upper hand. Try to flank enemies, use cover, and position your characters strategically. Remember to use healing potions or spells when necessary.

Manage your inventory

Your party members’ inventory can quickly become cluttered with various items. Make sure to manage your inventory frequently by selling or dropping items that are not useful. Keep an eye out for weapons, armor, and items that can enhance your characters’ abilities.

Talk to NPCs

Many non-playable characters (NPCs) in Solasta: Crown of the Magister offer quests, tips, or information about the game world. Talk to as many NPCs as possible to learn about the game world and uncover new quests.

Save often

Solasta: Crown of the Magister can be challenging, and you may encounter unexpected situations that require you to reload your game. Make sure to save your progress frequently to avoid losing progress.

These are just a few tips to get you started on your adventure in Solasta: Crown of the Magister. Remember to have fun, experiment with different strategies, and immerse yourself in the rich and detailed game world.

Solasta Crown of the Magister Gameplay

Solasta: Crown of the Magister is a turn-based tactical RPG that offers a rich and immersive gameplay experience. Players control a party of up to four characters, each with their own unique abilities, strengths, and weaknesses.

Exploration is a key component of Solasta’s gameplay. Players will journey through various environments, including caves, dungeons, and ruins, in search of treasure, clues, and quest objectives. As they explore, players will encounter a variety of enemies, from goblins and orcs to giant spiders and undead creatures.

Combat in Solasta is turn-based, with each character taking their turn based on their initiative score. Players can choose from a variety of actions, including attacks, spells, skills, and movement. Characters can use cover to avoid enemy attacks, and can also make use of height advantages to gain tactical advantages.

Magic is an important part of Solasta’s gameplay. Wizards can cast a variety of spells, including damage-dealing spells, buffs, debuffs, and utility spells. Clerics can heal their allies, banish undead, and even summon divine allies. Rogues can use their skills to disable traps and pick locks, while fighters can dish out massive damage and tank enemy attacks.

Players can customize their party members through a deep character creation system. They can choose from four races – human, dwarf, elf, and halfling – and four classes – fighter, rogue, cleric, and wizard. Each class has a variety of subclasses, allowing for even further customization.

Solasta’s gameplay is further enhanced by its realistic lighting and physics systems. Characters can use torches and light spells to illuminate dark areas, and can also make use of objects in the environment to gain advantages. For example, players can push crates or barrels to create cover, or use objects to block enemy movements.

Overall, Solasta: Crown of the Magister offers a rich and immersive gameplay experience that rewards careful planning, strategic thinking, and tactical mastery. With its deep character customization, varied combat system, and engaging exploration, Solasta is a must-play for fans of tactical RPGs.
