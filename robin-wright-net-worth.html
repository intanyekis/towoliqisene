What is Robin Wright’s net worth and salary?

Robin Wright, the American actress, has a net worth of $65 million dollars. She is best known for her role on the daytime soap opera “Santa Barbara,” and has received acclaim for her performances in movies such as “The Princess Bride,” “Forrest Gump,” “Unbreakable,” and “Nine Lives.”

Wright also won a Golden Globe Award for Best Actress for her role on the Netflix political drama “House of Cards.”

Divorce Settlement with Sean Penn

Robin Wright divorced actor Sean Penn in 2011 and reportedly received half of his fortune in the settlement. The estimated amount of the settlement was $40 million.

Robin Wright’s Earnings on “House of Cards”

During the height of “House of Cards,” Robin Wright brought in $9 million per season for her role in the hit series.

Early Life and Career

Robin Wright was born in 1966 in Dallas, Texas to Gayle, a cosmetics saleswoman, and Freddie, who worked at a pharmaceutical company. She grew up in San Diego, California, attending La Jolla High School and later, Taft High School in Los Angeles. At 14, she began her professional career as a model and later appeared in two episodes of “The Yellow Rose” in the early 1980s. Her breakthrough role came as Kelly Capwell on “Santa Barbara,” which aired from 1984 to 1988. Wright starred in 538 episodes of the show and earned multiple Daytime Emmy Award nominations for her performance.

Rising Star in Hollywood

In 1986, Robin Wright began her transition to feature films with a role in “Hollywood Vice Squad.” She went on to have her breakthrough movie role in the fantasy adventure comedy “The Princess Bride” in 1987. Wright starred in several films in the early 90s, including “Denial,” “State of Grace,” “The Playboys,” and “Toys.” However, her most critically acclaimed performance came in 1994 when she starred as Jenny Curran in “Forrest Gump,” for which she received Golden Globe and Screen Actors Guild nominations.

The following years saw Wright appear in a variety of dramas, including “The Crossing Guard,” “Moll Flanders,” “Loved,” and “She’s So Lovely.” She closed out the decade with roles in “Hurlyburly” and “Message in a Bottle.”

Robin Wright’s Acting Career

Robin Wright began the 2000s with roles in “How to Kill Your Neighbor’s Dog” and “Unbreakable.” She continued with parts in “The Pledge,” “White Oleander,” “The Singing Detective,” “Virgin,” and “A Home at the End of the World.” In 2005, Wright gave critically acclaimed performances in “Sorry, Haters” and “Nine Lives.” She also appeared in “Breaking and Entering,” “Hounddog,” “Beowulf,” “What Just Happened,” “State of Play,” and “The Private Lives of Pippa Lee.”

In the early 2010s, Wright had parts in “The Conspirator,” “Moneyball,” “Rampart,” and “The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo.” She was also in “Adore” and “The Congress,” and later appeared in “A Most Wanted Man,” “Everest,” “Wonder Woman,” “Blade Runner 2049,” and “Zack Snyder’s Justice League.” In 2021, Wright made her feature directorial debut with “Land,” in which she also starred.

Robin Wright’s Television Career

After 17 years away from television, Wright returned in the 2005 HBO miniseries “Empire Falls,” earning a Screen Actors Guild nomination. Her most acclaimed role came as Claire Underwood on Netflix’s “House of Cards,” earning multiple Emmy, SAG, and Golden Globe nominations and winning a Golden Globe in 2014. She became the lead of the show’s final season after Kevin Spacey’s firing, and her performance was highly acclaimed, garnering more nominations from the SAG and Emmy Awards.

Robin Wright’s Personal Life and Philanthropy

In 1986, Wright married actor Dane Witherspoon, whom she met on the set of “Santa Barbara.” They divorced in 1988. The following year, she began dating actor Sean Penn, whom she later married in 1991. They had two children before finalizing their divorce in 2010. Wright was engaged twice to actor Ben Foster, but they never wed. In 2018, she became engaged to Saint Laurent executive Clément Giraudet.

Wright is an honorary spokesperson for The Gordie Foundation, a nonprofit that aims to help young people deal with and prevent risks associated with alcohol consumption. She is also a human rights activist in the Democratic Republic of the Congo and advocates for issues in the nation.
