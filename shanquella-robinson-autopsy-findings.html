Unanswered Questions Surrounding Shanquella Robinson Autopsy Findings

The shocking details of Shanquella Robinson autopsy will leave you questioning everything you thought you knew about her death.

The FBI conducted a comprehensive investigation into the death of Shanquella Robinson and worked alongside the Robinson family to conduct an autopsy in Mecklenburg County, North Carolina. 

On Wednesday, federal officials met with the Robinson family and representatives to 

offer their condolences and present the findings of the investigation.

Attorney Robinson stated that the FBI interviewed Shanquella Robinson’s travel companions and individuals present in the house but did not disclose the number of people they interviewed.

The US officials informed the family that the autopsy was completed after Shanquella Robinson’s body was transported back to the US.

The Mecklenburg County Medical Examiner’s Office released the results of the Shanquella Robinson autopsy.

The medical examiner found some significant findings in the Shanquella Robinson autopsy results, but the cause of Robinson’s death remained unknown.

Shanquella Robinson’s autopsy revealed no spinal cord injury but showed swelling in her brain, though the cause of her death remains undetermined.

The report stated that there were no skull fractures or evidence of injury to the upper airway section, surrounding neck muscle, or cervical and upper thoracic vertebral column. 

There was also no evidence of any trauma or disease process in the organs. 

Shanquella Robinson autopsy report showed a minor scoliotic curvature in her upper-to-mid thoracic spine, but no other evidence of any disturbance or hemorrhage in her musculature, ligaments, or spinal elements. 

There were no fractures visible on the posterior side of her spinal column, and no indication of any disruption in the alignment or subluxation of her spinal column.

However, the report revealed a hematoma on Robinson’s forehead, mild cerebral edema, which is swelling of the brain due to injury, and hypoxic-ischemic brain injury, a condition in which there is an inadequate flow of blood and oxygen to the brain. 

They sent the brain, dura, and spinal cord for neuropathology consultation to investigate these findings further.

Sue-Ann Robinson criticized the US investigating agencies for delaying the investigation until their own autopsy was conducted. She argued that this allowed individuals to delete text messages and communicate with each other about the case.

She claimed there was no urgency on the part of US authorities to act quickly in this case.

Federal officials noted that they do not typically issue public statements regarding ongoing investigations, but did so in this instance due to the public’s concern about the case. 

Experienced federal agents and seasoned prosecutors carefully reviewed the evidence and concluded that they cannot pursue federal charges.

Mexican prosecutors previously announced that they were investigating Robinson’s death as a femicide, which is defined as the intentional murder of women because they are women.

On April 12, 2023, US federal prosecutors told Robinson’s family that the evidence they have is not enough for prosecution, leaving the family “very deeply disappointed.”

The attorney representing the family of Shanquella Robinson has announced that they are planning a march to the State Department headquarters in Washington, DC on May 19, which will mark the 200th day since Robinson’s death.

Robinson, a 25-year-old Black American hailing from North Carolina, passed away in October 2022 while staying in a luxurious rental property in Baja, California Sur, Mexico.

A video clip shared on social media showed an altercation in which Robinson was thrown to the ground and hit on the head. However, it remains unclear if this incident led to her fatal injury.
