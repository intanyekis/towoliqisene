Early Life

Megan Kelly, born on November 18, 1970, in Champaign, Illinois, had an upbringing in Syracuse, New York, in a Catholic household. Her early life played a significant role in shaping her career and values. She pursued her education at Syracuse University and Albany Law School, where she obtained an undergraduate degree in political science and a Juris Doctor degree.

“I come from a working-class family, and I’m proud of it. My father was an education professor, a double Ph.D. from Columbia. My mother stayed home and raised three of us. She was busy enough taking care of us, and that’s the way she liked it.” – Megan Kelly

Megan Kelly’s educational background and exposure to law and politics served as a solid foundation for her future endeavors in journalism and broadcasting. Career

Megan Kelly’s career in television began in 2003 when she became a news correspondent in Washington, D.C. Her talent and dedication quickly propelled her to success, leading her to join Fox News in 2004. At Fox News, Megan Kelly became a well-known television host and journalist, hosting popular shows such as “America Live” and “The Kelly File.”

With her charismatic presence and insightful reporting, Megan Kelly captivated audiences across the nation. She was known for her strong interviewing skills and ability to tackle tough subjects with grace and professionalism.

In 2017, Megan Kelly made a significant transition in her career when she moved to NBC News. However, her time at NBC News was marred by controversies, and she ultimately left the network in 2018. Despite these setbacks, Megan Kelly has continued to be a prominent figure in the media industry, leveraging her personal social media channels to reach her audience.
