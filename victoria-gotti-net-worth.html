Victoria Gotti is an American writer and television personality with a net worth of about $2 million. Born on November 27, 1962 in Brooklyn, New York, she is the daughter of the deceased Gambino crime family Mafia boss, John Gotti, who is also known as The Dapper Don and The Teflon Don. Together with her siblings John A. Gotti, Frank Gotti, Angel Gotti and Peter Gotti, they were brought up in Howard Beach. She grew up as an avid reader and devoted straight-A student who skipped two grades in high school. She graduated at the age of 15 and joined the St. Johns University in 1977.

She stated that while in St. Johns, she suffered from a condition known as mitral valve prolapse which makes the heart race causing palpitations and dizziness and she would occasionally wear a heart monitor. This health condition exempted her from strenuous academic commitments.

Victoria Gotti has earned her net worth through being a columnist at the New York Post and a reporter for WNYW, the New York City flagship station of the Fox Television Network. As a writer, her first book was Women and Mitral Valve Prolapse, which was inspired by and documented her own struggle with the illness. She also appeared along with her three sons in her reality show, Growing Up Gotti, an American reality television series on the A&E Network.
