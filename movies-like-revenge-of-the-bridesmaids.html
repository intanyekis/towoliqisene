12 Movies Like Revenge of the Bridesmaids You Must See

“Revenge of the Bridesmaids” is a romantic comedy that follows two friends, Abigail and Parker. When they visit their hometown, they discover that their best friend Rachel is a bridesmaid in the wedding of their childhood friend, Caitlyn, and Tony, who was Rachel’s boyfriend until Caitlyn interfered. Because of that, Abigail and Parker decide to run some interference of their own. They manipulate Caitlyn and become bridesmaids as well so that they and Rachel can sabotage the wedding from the inside. If you enjoyed this entertaining comedy, here are twelve movies similar to ‘Revenge of the Bridesmaids’ that are our recommendations.

12. My Fake Fiancé (2009)

“My Fake Fiancé” follows Jennifer (Melissa Joan Hart) and Vince (Joey Lawrence) who find themselves in need of cash and decide to stage a fake engagement and wedding just for the gifts. This family movie shows that when you marry, you’re not marrying one other person, you’re marrying their entire family. The lead characters do not understand that at the beginning, which leads to a lot of funny moments. Although it has some predictable moments, at the end you will be feeling good and happy.

11. Holiday Engagement (2011)

Afraid to tell her family she’s been dumped, Hillary (Bonnie Somerville) hires an actor to play her fiancé during a four-day Thanksgiving weekend at her parents’ house. “Holiday Engagement” is an enjoyable light movie for those craving a good romantic comedy. It is definitely one of those movies that will cheer you up and will make you feel good. The chemistry between Jordan Bridges and Bonnie Somerville is quite believable.

10. The Wedding Planner (2001)

Mary Fiore is an ambitious, hard-working wedding planner. But when she falls in love with a handsome doctor her busy life is turned upside down because he is the groom in the biggest wedding of her career. Although not a fresh and original story, “The Wedding Planner” is still an entertaining movie with some Hollywood stars – Jennifer Lopez and Matthew McConaughey. By creating a story focusing on its two main characters, this movie provides amusing situations that will make you laugh and cry. A great choice for those looking for a romance movie.

9. The Big Wedding (2013)

“The Big Wedding” follows a long-divorced couple that has to fake being married at their adopted son’s wedding. The cast of “The Big Wedding” is phenomenal: Robert De Niro, Diane Keaton, Susan Sarandon, Robin Williams, Amanda Seyfried and Katherine Heigl. You can feel that they all worked really hard to make this film because of their fine performances. Besides being a wedding movie made around sex jokes, this movie has some honest family drama. These two aspects make this one very original and entertaining.

8. Bachelorette (2012)

“Bachelorette” is about three friends who are asked to be bridesmaids at a wedding of a woman they used to ridicule back in high school. This movie is a dark comedy where all the characters are really complex, especially the women. “Bachelorette” will not only remind you of “Revenge of the Bridesmaids” but also of “Mean Girls”, “Hangover” and “Bridesmaids”. Additionally, this movie has a powerful, featuring Kristen Dunst, Rebel Wilson, Lizzy Caplan and Isla Fisher.

7. 27 Dresses (2008)

Jane served as a bridesmaid 27 times. However, now she is struggling with the idea of standing by her sister’s side as her sibling marries the man she’s secretly in love with. “27 Dresses” is a fun romantic comedy despite being predictable at times. Katherine Heigl manages to put in good acting to portray her jealousy towards her sister. Also, she makes the viewers connect with her, which is very important. Additionally, Judy Greer is brilliant, and she owns all her scenes. The cast has an amazing chemistry which gives a unique charm and sense of humor to the whole movie.

6. One Small Hitch (2013)

On a flight home to Chicago for a family wedding, childhood friends Josh and Molly innocently agree to fake a wedding engagement to make Josh’s dying father happy. However, things quickly get out of control, which causes them to pretend to be a couple and start planning a phony wedding. “One Small Hitch” has some great lead characters and actors that have good chemistry as friends and lovers. Its story is cute and funny, focusing on all kinds of relationships (lovers, friends and family) which really refreshing. Those who love Chicago will especially appreciate this movie since it has several homages and references to the city.

5. Maid of Honor (2008)

This classic romantic movie follows Tom and Hannah, who have been platonic friends for 10 years. Suddenly, Hannah gets engaged. When she asks Tom to be her “maid” of honor, he reluctantly agrees just so he can attempt to stop the wedding and confess his love to her. “Maid of Honor” is not groundbreaking but it is quite refreshing to see a romantic movie from the perspective of a man. If you enjoy romantic comedies and want a good laugh this one is definitely worth seeing. Patrick Dempsey and Michelle Monaghan are great in the skin of their characters and they have great chemistry together.

4. My Best Friend’s Wedding (1997)

When a woman’s long-time friend reveals he’s engaged, she realizes she loves him herself and sets out to get him, with only days before the wedding. With a great soundtrack and exciting plot, “My Best Friend’s Wedding” became an instant classic in 1997. Julia Roberts gets a great opportunity to showcase her talent, and she does so. Her natural charm comes through in a fantastic way. Also, Cameron Diaz and Rupert Everett are quite good.

3. Bride Wars (2009)

Two best friends (Kate Hudson and Anne Hathaway) become rivals when they schedule their respective weddings on the same day. Besides being all about weddings and celebrating love, it follows a ruined friendship because of competition. Best friends can become your worst enemies since they know every little secret you had confided in them. “Bride Wars” is the perfect chick flick with an interesting idea and amazing Hollywood stars. It not only has some great jokes and hilarious moments but also has a cast that does not take itself too seriously. Hathaway and Hudson are great together, you will laugh out loud a lot while watching “Bride Wars”.

2. The Other Woman (2014)

After discovering her boyfriend is married, Carly soon learns that he is trying to maintain three separate relationships. All three women team up to plot revenge on him. By exploring the meaning of true sisterhood in unusual circumstances, “The Other Woman” becomes an entertaining film about a group of resilient, strong women. Cameron Diaz, Leslie Mann and Kate Upton were very good in their individual roles and the chemistry between them was strong. An excellent film to relax on a Sunday afternoon.

1. Bridesmaids (2011)

Annie (Kristen Wiig) is a maid of honor whose life loosens as she leads her best friend, Lillian (Maya Rudolph), and a group of colorful bridesmaids (Rose Byrne, Melissa McCarthy, Wendi McLendon-Covey and Ellie Kemper) on a wild ride on the road to the wedding. With an incredibly talented cast, “Bridesmaids” is an extremely funny movie that challenges formula movies and stereotypes. Kristen Wiig and Annie Mumolo managed to write a hilarious comedy about a competition between the maid of honor and a bridesmaid, over who is the bride’s best friend.
